A — Homepage Does The Heavy Lifting

The homepage tells the entire FN3 story in one scroll. Sub-pages provide depth for those who want more. Most visitors get everything they need without leaving the home page. Best for conviction-building across all audiences simultaneously.

Pros

  • One scroll = full picture
  • Less navigation friction
  • Homepage becomes the brand

Cons

  • Longer homepage to maintain
  • Sub-pages feel thinner

B — Homepage as Portal, Pages as Experiences

Homepage is short — just identity and navigation intent. Each sub-page is a full, rich experience for that audience. Investor clicks Portfolio. Client clicks Services. Partner clicks About. The homepage trusts the visitor to know what they want.

Pros

  • Clean separation of audiences
  • Each page optimized for one purpose
  • Fast homepage load

Cons

  • Requires strong navigation UX
  • Homepage alone doesn't convince

Navigation language is intentional — human, not corporate. Each chapter is self-contained but part of a sequence.

C — Chapters (Narrative Navigation)

The nav itself tells a story. "What We Are → What We've Built → How We Work → Work With Us" is a logical argument in four steps. Every audience enters the same story at different points. The naming does positioning work before they even click.

Pros

  • Nav = micro-manifesto
  • Clear logical flow for all audiences
  • Pages feel intentional, not generic

Cons

  • Chapter names need careful writing
  • Slightly unconventional — needs confident execution

My recommendation is C — but let me know what resonates. We can mix too.
