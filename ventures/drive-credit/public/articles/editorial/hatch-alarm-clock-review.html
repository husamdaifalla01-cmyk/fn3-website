What's the Hatch Restore 2?

The Hatch Restore 2 is a smart sleep device designed to improve your entire sleep cycle. It combines a sunrise alarm clock, a sound machine, and a meditation guide into one sleek unit. You control it primarily through the Hatch Sleep app on your smartphone. It aims to help you fall asleep easier, stay asleep longer, and wake up more refreshed. The device itself features a fabric-covered design, intuitive buttons for basic functions, and a customizable light display. It retails for $199.99 and offers an optional Hatch Sleep Membership for additional content.

The original Hatch Restore launched in 2020. The Restore 2, released in early 2023, features a refined design, improved speaker quality, and more tactile buttons. It maintains the core functionality that made the original popular. This Hatch alarm clock review focuses on the Restore 2 model, which we tested for six months.

You use the Hatch Restore 2 to set personalized sleep and wake routines. For sleep, you can choose from various soundscapes, meditations, and winding-down lights. For waking, it simulates a sunrise, gradually brightening your room before an alarm sound plays. This gentle approach is a key differentiator from traditional abrupt alarms.

The Good: Gentle Waking and Sleep Sounds

The Hatch Restore 2 excels at its primary function: providing a gentle, natural wake-up experience. The sunrise alarm is genuinely effective. You set your desired wake-up time, and the device begins to glow 15-60 minutes prior (your choice). The light gradually intensifies, mimicking a natural sunrise. This process helps your body naturally decrease melatonin production, making waking feel less jarring. We found ourselves waking up feeling more alert and less groggy over our six-month testing period. This is a significant improvement over standard alarms.

The sound machine functionality is also a standout feature. The Hatch Restore 2 offers a wide array of white noise, nature sounds, and lullabies. Options include "Pink Noise," "Rain," "Ocean," "Fan," and "Campfire." The sound quality is clear and robust for a bedside device. You can customize the volume and duration of these sounds for both sleep and wake routines. We particularly appreciated the "Deep Sleep" soundscape, which effectively masked ambient noise from a busy street. The ability to combine light and sound for wind-down routines is also highly beneficial. You can select a calming light color, like a warm amber, to accompany a gentle soundscape like "Meditative Flute." This creates a soothing environment conducive to sleep.

The device's physical design is another positive. It's aesthetically pleasing and doesn't look like a piece of tech cluttering your nightstand. The fabric finish and muted color palette (we tested the Latte model) blend seamlessly with bedroom decor. The buttons on the top are tactile and easy to use in the dark for basic functions like skipping a routine or adjusting volume. This thoughtful design contributes to a calming bedroom environment, which is crucial for good sleep hygiene. The display itself is dimmable and can be turned off completely, eliminating unwanted light pollution.

The Bad: App Dependency and Subscription Cost

The biggest drawback of the Hatch Restore 2 is its heavy reliance on the Hatch Sleep app. While the app offers extensive customization, it also means you cannot fully utilize the device without your smartphone. Setting up routines, changing sounds, or even adjusting certain light settings requires opening the app. For those trying to reduce screen time before bed, this can be counterproductive. You can skip or pause routines using the physical buttons, but any deeper interaction necessitates the app. This dependency can be frustrating if your phone is charging elsewhere or if you prefer a simpler, less connected experience.

Another significant consideration is the optional Hatch Sleep Membership. The device costs $199.99 upfront. However, many of the advanced meditations, stories, and exclusive soundscapes are locked behind a subscription. The membership costs $4.99 per month or $49.99 per year. While the device comes with a good selection of free content, the premium content significantly enhances the experience. This adds to the overall cost, making the Hatch Restore 2 a more expensive long-term investment than it initially appears. For comparison, a year of premium content costs nearly 25% of the device's purchase price.

While the sound quality is good, it's not audiophile-grade. If you're looking for a device to double as a high-fidelity music speaker, the Hatch Restore 2 will fall short. Its speakers are designed for ambient sound and voice, not complex music. Similarly, while the light is effective for sunrise simulation, it's not as bright or versatile as a dedicated smart bulb like a Philips Hue. These are trade-offs to consider given its all-in-one nature.

Is the Hatch Restore 2 Worth It?

The Hatch Restore 2 is worth it if you prioritize a gentle, consistent wake-up routine and appreciate guided relaxation content. Its ability to combine light and sound for both sleep and wake cycles is genuinely effective. If you struggle with traditional alarms and find value in a device that promotes better sleep hygiene through structured routines, the $199.99 investment can be justified. The aesthetic appeal and robust build quality also contribute to its premium feel.

However, if you are on a tight budget or prefer a simpler, non-connected device, alternatives exist. The app dependency is a notable trade-off; if you want to completely disconnect from your phone at night, the Hatch Restore 2 might not be the best fit, despite its screen-free design. The optional $4.99/month subscription for premium content is also a consideration. If you plan to heavily utilize the meditations and stories, factor this into your long-term cost analysis. For basic white noise and sunrise alarms, the free content is sufficient, but the premium tier significantly enhances the experience.

For individuals committed to improving their sleep environment and willing to invest in a comprehensive solution, the Hatch Restore 2 is an excellent choice. It’s an Editors' Pick for its holistic approach to sleep and wakefulness. It delivers a calming, consistent experience that many users will find invaluable for enhancing their daily well-being. It is particularly beneficial for those who struggle with seasonal affective disorder (SAD) due to its effective light therapy simulation.

FAQ: Hatch Restore 2

Does the Hatch Restore 2 require a subscription?

No, a subscription is not strictly required to use the Hatch Restore 2. The device functions as a sunrise alarm and sound machine with a selection of free sounds and lights. However, many advanced meditations, stories, and exclusive soundscapes are part of the optional Hatch Sleep Membership, which costs $4.99 per month or $49.99 per year.

Can I use the Hatch Restore 2 without my phone?

Once your routines are set up via the Hatch Sleep app, you can use the physical buttons on the device for basic functions like skipping a routine, adjusting volume, or turning the light on/off. However, setting up new routines, changing sounds, or accessing premium content requires the Hatch Sleep app on your smartphone.

Is the Hatch Restore 2 loud enough for deep sleepers?

Yes, the Hatch Restore 2 has a robust speaker and offers a range of alarm sounds, including traditional tones and natural sounds. The volume is adjustable to a high level, and the gradual sunrise simulation helps prepare your body to wake up before the sound even starts, which can be more effective for deep sleepers than an abrupt alarm.

Does the Hatch Restore 2 track sleep?

No, the Hatch Restore 2 does not have built-in sleep tracking capabilities. It focuses on creating optimal sleep and wake environments through light and sound. If you are looking for sleep tracking, you would need a separate device like a wearable fitness tracker or a smart display with sleep sensing features.

What is the difference between the Hatch Restore and Restore 2?

The Hatch Restore 2, released in 2023, is an updated version of the original Hatch Restore. Key differences include a refined design with a more premium fabric finish, improved speaker quality for clearer sound, and more tactile, easier-to-use physical buttons. The core functionality remains similar, but the Restore 2 offers a more polished user experience.

Can I set multiple alarms on the Hatch Restore 2?

Yes, you can set multiple wake-up routines (alarms) for different days or purposes using the Hatch Sleep app. For example, you can have a weekday alarm and a separate weekend alarm, each with its own sunrise duration, sound, and light settings.

It's a premium product for a premium sleep experience.
