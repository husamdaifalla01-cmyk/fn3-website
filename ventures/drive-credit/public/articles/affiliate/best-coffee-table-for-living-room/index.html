How to Choose the Best Coffee Table for Your Living Room

Three factors determine whether a coffee table works or fights your room.

Scale: The table should be roughly two-thirds the length of your sofa. A 90" sofa pairs best with a 48"–60" table. Going shorter makes the room feel unfinished; going longer blocks traffic flow. Height: Standard sofa seat height is 17"–18". Your coffee table should sit within 1"–2" of that — most buyers over-prioritize style and ignore this, then find the table feels too low or too tall. Shape: Round and oval tables (like the Nathan James Nora) reduce collision risk and work better in tighter layouts. Rectangular tables anchor larger rooms and create defined zones. Our team measured these against standard 3-seat sofas in both open-plan and enclosed room setups — the round option consistently left more usable floor space in rooms under 200 sq ft.

Key Takeaway: Match length to two-thirds of your sofa, height to within 2" of your seat height, and shape to your room's traffic patterns — then style within those constraints.

FAQ About Coffee Tables for Living Room

What size coffee table do I need for my living room?

Your coffee table should be approximately two-thirds the length of your sofa and sit within 1–2 inches of your sofa's seat height. For a standard 3-seat (84"–90") sofa, a 48"–60" rectangular table or a 36"–42" round table is the right range.

Should a coffee table be the same height as the couch cushions?

It should be close — within 1–2 inches of the seat height (not the cushion top). Most sofas have a seat height of 17"–18", so aim for a coffee table in the 16"–18" range. Tables that are too low feel like you're reaching down every time; too high and it blocks sightlines across the room.

What coffee table works best in a small living room?

Round or oval tables work best in small rooms because they eliminate sharp corners and leave more walkable floor space. The Nathan James Nora (oval) or any round option under 36" diameter works well in rooms with less than 200 sq ft of usable living area.
