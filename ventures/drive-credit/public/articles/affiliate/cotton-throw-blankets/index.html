Common Mistakes When Buying Organic Cotton Throws

You want to make a smart purchase. Avoid these pitfalls to ensure you get the most out of your organic cotton throw.

  • Ignoring certification labels: Don't just trust "organic" claims. Look for certifications like GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard). This ensures the cotton is organic from harvesting through manufacturing, and that ethical labor practices are followed. Without GOTS, "organic cotton" can sometimes be misleading.
  • Not checking dimensions: A "throw" can range significantly in size. Some are small accent pieces (e.g., 50x60 inches), while others are large enough to cover the foot of a queen bed (e.g., 60x80 inches). Measure your space and consider how you intend to use the blanket to ensure it's the right fit.
  • Overlooking care instructions: Organic cotton can be durable, but proper care extends its life. Many organic cotton throws recommend cold water wash and tumble dry low to prevent shrinking, fading, or damage to the fibers. High heat can be particularly harsh on natural fibers.
  • Prioritizing price over quality (or vice versa): While budget is important, the cheapest option might not offer the longevity or comfort you desire. Conversely, the most expensive isn't always the best for your specific needs. Balance your budget with the features and quality that matter most to you. Sometimes spending a little more upfront saves you money in the long run by avoiding frequent replacements.
  • Forgetting about texture and weave: "Organic cotton" describes the material, not the feel. A percale weave will feel crisp, a sateen weave will feel silky, and a waffle weave will feel textured and substantial. Consider what kind of tactile experience you prefer against your skin.

Q

What does GOTS certified mean?

A

GOTS stands for Global Organic Textile Standard. It's the world's leading processing standard for textile fibers made from organic materials. It ensures that the entire supply chain, from harvesting the raw materials to environmentally and socially responsible manufacturing, is organic and ethical. When you see GOTS certified, you know it's the real deal.

Q

Is organic cotton really worth the extra cost?

A

Yes, for several reasons. Organic cotton is grown without harmful pesticides, herbicides, or synthetic fertilizers. This is better for the environment, for the farmers, and for your skin. It often results in a softer, more durable product because the fibers aren't exposed to harsh chemicals. While it might cost more upfront, you're investing in a product that's healthier for you and the planet, and often lasts longer.

Q

How often should I wash my organic cotton throw?

A

It depends on how frequently you use it and whether it comes into direct contact with skin. For a decorative throw, every 1-2 months might be sufficient. If you use it daily and it's your go-to snuggle blanket, washing it every 2-4 weeks is a good idea. Always follow the specific care instructions on the label to maintain its quality.
