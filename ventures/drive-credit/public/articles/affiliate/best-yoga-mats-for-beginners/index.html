What Beginners Should Look For

Thickness: 6–10mm for joint protection while you're building practice. Thinner mats (3–5mm) are for experienced practitioners who prioritize feel over cushion.

Grip: Test in a downward dog. If your hands slide, you'll compensate with tension that causes injury. Textured or polyurethane surfaces outperform smooth foam.

Length: Standard is 68 inches. If you're over 5'10", get a 72-inch mat. Feet hanging off creates misalignment habits early.

FAQ

Do I need a yoga mat to start practicing at home?
Yes — any hard floor surface without grip creates injury risk in weight-bearing poses. Even the budget Amazon Basics pick is significantly safer than no mat.
How do I clean a yoga mat?
Wipe with a damp cloth after each session. For a deeper clean, mix 1 part white vinegar to 4 parts water and let air dry completely before rolling. Avoid machine washing foam mats — they lose structure.
How long should a yoga mat last?
Budget mats: 6–12 months of regular use. Mid-range (Gaiam, Manduka eKO Lite): 2–3 years. Premium (Lululemon, Liforme): 5+ years. The per-use cost often favors buying better.
