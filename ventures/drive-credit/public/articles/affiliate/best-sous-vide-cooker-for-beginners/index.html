How to Choose Your First Sous Vide Cooker

When choosing your first sous vide cooker, you should focus on ease of use, wattage, and connectivity to ensure a smooth introduction to this cooking method.

You might be wondering what really matters when you're just starting out. Here's what we recommend you consider:

  • Ease of Use: Look for models with intuitive controls, whether that's through a simple on-device interface or a user-friendly app. For beginners, an app can be incredibly helpful with guided recipes and temperature settings.
  • Wattage: While not the primary concern for a beginner, wattage affects how quickly your water bath heats up. A 750W unit like the Anova Nano is perfectly adequate for most home cooking, but if you anticipate cooking larger volumes or want faster heating, a 1000W unit like the INKBIRD might be worth the slight price increase.
  • Connectivity (WiFi/Bluetooth): App control is a huge convenience for beginners. It allows you to monitor and adjust your cook from another room, and often provides access to a wealth of recipes and cooking guides directly on your phone.
  • Size and Storage: Consider your kitchen space. Compact designs are great for smaller kitchens or if you plan to store the unit away when not in use.
  • Price: As a beginner, you don't need to splurge on the most expensive model. Both the Anova Nano at $62.72 and the INKBIRD at $95.99 offer excellent value and performance for their price points.
Key Takeaway: Prioritize ease of use (especially app control), consider wattage for heating speed, and ensure the unit fits your kitchen and budget when selecting your first sous vide cooker.
