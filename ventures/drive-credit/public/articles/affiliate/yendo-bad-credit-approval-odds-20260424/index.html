What is a credit card backed by your car title, and how does it work?

A credit card backed by your car title, like Yendo, uses your vehicle as collateral. This means that instead of relying solely on your credit history, the lender has a security interest in your car. If you default on payments, the lender could potentially repossess the car to recover the debt. This might sound intimidating, but for many, it's the only path to getting approved for credit when traditional avenues are closed.

Here’s how it typically works: You apply for the Yendo credit card. During the application process, Yendo assesses the value of your car. If your car meets their criteria (it’s paid off and has sufficient value), and you meet their other basic requirements, you may be approved for a credit limit based on a percentage of your car's value. You continue to drive your car as usual. The car title is simply held by Yendo as collateral until the card is paid off or the account is closed. This model allows Yendo to offer credit to individuals with poor or no credit history, as the risk is mitigated by the collateral.

It’s important to understand that this is a serious financial commitment. Just like any secured loan or credit card, you must make your payments on time. However, for those who have been shut out of the financial system, this type of secured credit card can be a lifeline, providing access to credit and, crucially, a path to rebuilding credit.
