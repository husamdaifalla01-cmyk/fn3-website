How to Choose a Clean Foundation as a Beginner

Start by deciding what matters most: ease of application, coverage level, or ingredient purity. For absolute beginners, we recommend starting with a spray or liquid foundation that requires minimal tools. Look for formulas labeled "mineral" or "100% natural" if you're concerned about toxins. Check the shade range—Clinique offers 20+ shades, while Harvest Natural has fewer. Read reviews from other beginners to gauge ease of use. And always patch test before full application.

Is Jerome Alexander or bareMinerals better for first-time clean makeup users?

For first-time clean makeup users, Jerome Alexander is better because of its spray application—no brushes or sponges needed. bareMinerals offers full coverage and 16-hour wear, but requires a buffing technique that may be trickier for beginners.

What does "clean foundation" mean?

A clean foundation typically means it's free from synthetic chemicals, parabens, phthalates, and other potentially harmful ingredients. Brands like Harvest Natural Beauty use 100% natural ingredients, while others like Clinique focus on dermatologist-tested, non-irritating formulas.

How do I apply a mineral foundation as a beginner?

For a spray mineral foundation like Jerome Alexander, hold the can 6-8 inches from your face, spray in a circular motion, and blend with your fingers or a sponge. For powder mineral foundations like bareMinerals, use a dense kabuki brush in circular motions for even coverage.

Are clean foundations more expensive?
