Frequently Asked Questions About Linen Bedding

Is linen bedding good for hot sleepers?

Yes, linen bedding is exceptionally good for hot sleepers because it is naturally breathable and temperature-regulating, wicking away moisture to keep you cool and comfortable.

What's the difference between linen and cotton bedding?

Linen is made from flax fibers, known for its superior strength, breathability, and ability to get softer with each wash, while cotton, made from cotton plant fibers, is generally softer initially and more widely available, though less effective at temperature regulation than linen.

How to care for linen duvet cover?

To care for a linen duvet cover, machine wash it on a gentle cycle with cold water and a mild detergent, then tumble dry on low heat or line dry to preserve its fibers and softness.

Why is linen bedding often more expensive than cotton?

Linen bedding is typically more expensive due to the labor-intensive cultivation and processing of flax fibers, which are more difficult to harvest and weave than cotton, contributing to its higher production cost and durability.

What are the benefits of sleeping with linen bedding?

Sleeping with linen bedding offers numerous benefits, including enhanced breathability, natural temperature regulation, increased durability, hypoallergenic properties, and a luxurious feel that softens over time.
