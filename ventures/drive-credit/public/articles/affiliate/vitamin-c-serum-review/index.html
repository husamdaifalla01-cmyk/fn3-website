  • Using an oxidized serum: Vitamin C, especially L-Ascorbic Acid, is unstable. It oxidizes when exposed to air, light, and heat. It turns yellow, then orange, then brown. An oxidized serum is ineffective and can even generate free radicals. Always check the color. Store it in a cool, dark place, ideally your fridge. Finish it within 3-4 months of opening.
  • Layering incorrectly: Apply your Vitamin C serum on clean, dry skin. This allows for maximum absorption. Follow with other serums, then moisturizer, and always SPF in the morning. Don't mix it directly with niacinamide or retinol unless your skin is very tolerant and you understand the potential for irritation. Apply Vitamin C in the morning for antioxidant protection against daily environmental aggressors.
  • Skipping SPF: Vitamin C makes your skin more susceptible to sun damage if you don't use SPF. It’s an antioxidant, but it’s not a sunscreen. You must use a broad-spectrum SPF 30+ every single day. This protects your skin and allows the Vitamin C to do its job of repairing and brightening, not just fighting new damage.
  • Expecting instant results: Skincare takes time. You won't see dramatic changes overnight. Give your serum at least 4-6 weeks of consistent use to show results. Full benefits often appear after 8-12 weeks. Be patient and consistent.
  • Using too high a concentration too soon: If you're new to Vitamin C, start with a lower concentration (5-10%). This helps your skin adjust. Higher concentrations (15-20%) can be more effective but also more irritating. Build up gradually if needed.
  • Not patching testing: Always test a new product on a small area of skin first. Apply a small amount behind your ear or on your inner arm for a few days. This helps you check for irritation or allergic reactions before applying it to your entire face.

Q

Can I use Vitamin C serum every day?

A

Yes, you can use Vitamin C serum daily, typically in the morning. Some people with sensitive skin might start with every other day and gradually increase frequency.

Q

Will Vitamin C serum make my skin more sensitive to the sun?

A

No, Vitamin C itself does not make your skin more sensitive to the sun. In fact, it provides antioxidant protection against UV damage. However, you absolutely must still use a broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily to protect your skin and maximize the benefits of the serum.

Q

How long does a bottle of Vitamin C serum last?

A

A typical 30ml bottle of Vitamin C serum, used daily, usually lasts 2-3 months. Because Vitamin C can oxidize, it's best to finish the bottle within 3-4 months of opening, even if there's still some left, to ensure maximum efficacy.
