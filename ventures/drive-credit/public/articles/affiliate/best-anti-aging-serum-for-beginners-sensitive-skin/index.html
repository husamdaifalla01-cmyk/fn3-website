How to Choose Your First Anti-Aging Serum

Choosing your first anti-aging serum can feel daunting, but focusing on your specific skin concerns and sensitivity level will guide you to the right product.

As a beginner, you might be unsure whether to start with retinol, vitamin C, or another active ingredient. The key is to identify your primary goal. Are you most concerned about fine lines and texture, like with the La Roche-Posay Pure Retinol Face Serum? Or is brightening dullness and dark spots your priority, which the La Roche-Posay Pure 12% Vitamin C Serum excels at? If enlarged pores are your main issue, then Paula's Choice CLINICAL 20% Niacinamide might be a better fit. Always start with a lower concentration and introduce new products slowly, perhaps just a few times a week, to allow your skin to adjust and minimize potential irritation. Look for dermatologist-tested formulas and ingredients like hyaluronic acid or niacinamide which can help buffer the effects of stronger actives. Don't feel pressured to use multiple new products at once; a single, targeted serum is often the best approach for a beginner.
