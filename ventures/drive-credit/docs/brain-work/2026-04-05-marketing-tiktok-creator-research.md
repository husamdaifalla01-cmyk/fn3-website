## Top Creator Archetypes in Bad-Credit TikTok

### 1. The "Financial Hack" Creator
**Pattern:** Short, punchy reveals of "secrets" the system hides
**Hooks that work:**
- "Nobody tells you this about credit cards..."
- "The bank doesn't want you to know..."
- "DELETE this before they take it down"
**Why it works:** Info-gap + anti-establishment energy. Audience feels like they're getting insider knowledge.
**Risk for us:** Can veer into misleading territory. Must stay FTC compliant.
**Adapt for Mintbrooks:** Use the curiosity gap but deliver real, specific value. "Your car is worth $X in credit" is genuinely useful info, not hype.

### 2. The "Story of Redemption" Creator
**Pattern:** Personal narrative of going from bad credit to improvement
**Hooks that work:**
- "I went from 480 to 720 in 18 months"
- "The bank said no. Here's what I did instead."
- "I was $30k in debt at 24. Here's my plan."
**Why it works:** Relatability + hope. Audience sees themselves in the story.
**Risk for us:** Can't fabricate stories. Husam should tell genuine experiences.
**Adapt for Mintbrooks:** "I found a credit card that doesn't look at your score" is a genuine discovery story.

### 3. The "Quick Tip" Educator
**Pattern:** Numbered lists, actionable tips, under 30 seconds
**Hooks that work:**
- "3 things I wish I knew about credit"
- "Credit tip nobody talks about"
- "Do this before applying for a credit card"
**Why it works:** High save rate. People bookmark educational content.
**Risk for us:** Lower virality than emotional hooks.
**Adapt for Mintbrooks:** Use as secondary content type. "5 things about car-equity credit" format.

### 4. The "Reaction" Creator
**Pattern:** Reacting to other financial content, stitching bad advice
**Hooks that work:**
- "This guy is WRONG about credit cards"
- Stitch: "Here's what they didn't tell you"
- Duet: Side-by-side correction
**Why it works:** Drama + education. Algorithm loves stitches/duets.
**Risk for us:** Need existing content to react to. Good for weeks 3+.
**Adapt for Mintbrooks:** React to bad credit advice with car-equity as the better alternative.

### 5. The "POV" Creator
**Pattern:** POV scenarios the audience relates to
**Hooks that work:**
- "POV: You just got denied for the 5th time"
- "POV: You found out your car is worth $8k in credit"
- "POV: The bank teller looks at your credit score"
**Why it works:** Emotional identification. Audience projects themselves into the scenario.
**Risk for us:** Need good acting. Husam should lean into genuine frustration/relief.
**Adapt for Mintbrooks:** Already have POV script in Batch 2. Lean harder into this format.
