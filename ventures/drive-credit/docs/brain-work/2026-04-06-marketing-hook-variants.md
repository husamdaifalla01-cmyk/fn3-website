## The Single Insight Behind All Hooks

Our ICP has been rejected by banks. They've been told "no" so many times they assume financial products aren't for them. Our hook job is to **break their resignation** — make them think "wait, that might actually apply to me."

The second job of every hook: make them feel like they're getting information the system tried to hide from them.

---

## 5 Hook Variants (Ready for Remotion + Filming)

### Hook 1: The Pattern Interrupt (EMBARRASSMENT)
**On-screen text:** "Owning a car and not doing this is lowkey embarrassing"
**Voiceover:** "If you own a car and have bad credit, there's a financial product made specifically for you. Most people don't know it exists. In 30 seconds I'll show you exactly what it is."
**Category:** Curiosity / self-awareness
**Why it works:** "Embarrassing" triggers ego — the viewer feels they're about to learn something everyone else already knows. High scroll-stop.
**CTA:** "Link in bio — check if your car qualifies. Takes 30 seconds."

---

### Hook 2: The Specific Number (CREDIBILITY)
**On-screen text:** "$10,000 credit limit. No credit score required. Your car."
**Voiceover:** "Here's a number that surprises most people. You can get a $500 to $10,000 Visa credit card just by owning a car. Not a title loan. Not a payday loan. A real Visa credit card. And they don't check your credit score to qualify."
**Category:** Proof / specificity
**Why it works:** Specific numbers beat vague claims every time. "$10,000" and "no credit score" are the two facts that change behavior.
**CTA:** "Link in bio — see what your car's worth in credit."

---

### Hook 3: The Villain (ANGER)
**On-screen text:** "Banks invented the credit score to keep you out."
**Voiceover:** "The credit score system was designed in 1956 by a company that profits from lenders using it. If you have bad credit, you're not a bad person — you're trapped in a system that was never designed to let you in. But there's a workaround. Your car."
**Category:** Agitation / systemic anger
**Why it works:** Externalizes blame from the viewer to the system. They feel validated, not judged. Sets up the product as the "escape."
**CTA:** "Link in bio — the workaround they don't teach you."

---

### Hook 4: The Before/After (TRANSFORMATION)
**On-screen text:** "Before: rejected by 3 banks. After: $7,000 Visa card."
**Voiceover:** "This is how car-secured credit works. Your car is worth money — even if the bank doesn't think you are. Most cars qualify for a $500 to $10,000 credit limit. Soft inquiry, no deposit, keep driving. The eligibility check takes 30 seconds."
**Category:** Transformation / proof
**Why it works:** Before/after is the most powerful storytelling format in direct response. Specific numbers anchor the claim.
**CTA:** "Link in bio — check what your car unlocks."

---

### Hook 5: The Objection Preempt (TRUST)
**On-screen text:** "Is this a scam? Honest answer:"
**Voiceover:** "I get it. A credit card backed by your car with no credit check sounds too good to be true. So let me be direct. It's a real licensed fintech company. Real Visa card. Real credit limit. The catch: they put a lien on your title — not a title loan, a lien. You keep driving. If you pay your bill, nothing happens to your car. If you don't, it's like any debt default."
**Category:** Objection handling / transparency
**Why it works:** Preemptively handling the "is this a scam" thought builds massive trust. Radical honesty on cons is counterintuitively the most persuasive approach.
**CTA:** "Link in bio — decide for yourself if it's right for you."
