## New FAQ Entries (for mintbrooks.com and support docs)

### Q: My car still has a loan on it. Can I still apply for Yendo?
**A:** In most cases, Yendo requires a clear vehicle title — meaning your car is fully paid off with no existing lien. If you still have an active auto loan, you likely won't qualify because Yendo needs to hold first-lien position on the title. The exception is if you have a car that's almost paid off and you pay it off before applying — in that case, you'd then have a free-and-clear title. Always check directly with Yendo for your specific situation, as policies can change.

### Q: What happens to my car if I miss a payment?
**A:** Missing a payment on your Yendo card does NOT mean your car gets repossessed immediately — this isn't a title loan. Like any credit card, late payments will affect your credit score and trigger late fees and interest charges. However, if you default significantly (non-payment for extended periods), Yendo does hold a lien on the title, which could eventually lead to repossession as a last resort. Treat it like any credit card — make minimum payments on time, and your car is completely safe.

### Q: How is Yendo different from a title loan?
**A:** Night and day different. A title loan gives you a lump sum of cash (usually 25–50% of your car's value) with high interest rates (often 100–300% APR) and a short repayment window. Miss a payment and they repossess your car. Yendo is a revolving credit card — you get a credit limit, buy what you need, and pay it back monthly. The interest rate is credit-card-level, not predatory. And missing one payment doesn't cost you your car. It's a credit-building tool, not a desperation product.

### Q: Will using Yendo help improve my credit score?
**A:** Yes, meaningfully. Yendo reports to all three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) every month. If you make on-time payments and keep your utilization below 30% of your limit, you should see score improvements within 3–6 months. For thin-file borrowers (little to no credit history), a Yendo card can be especially impactful — it adds a new revolving account with regular positive payment history, which is the biggest factor in your score.

### Q: Can I use the Yendo card at any store?
**A:** Yes — it's a Visa card, which means it works at the 46+ million locations worldwide that accept Visa. Online stores, physical stores, restaurants, gas stations, subscriptions — anything that accepts Visa. There's no restriction on where you use it (unlike a store-specific credit card). You can also use it for automatic payments to further build your credit history.
