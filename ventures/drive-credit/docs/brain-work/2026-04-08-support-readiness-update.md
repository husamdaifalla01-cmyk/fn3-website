## New FAQ Additions (based on Reddit research this cycle)

**Q: What happens if I miss a payment with Yendo?**
> Late fees apply per Yendo's cardholder agreement. If you miss multiple payments, Yendo may report the delinquency to credit bureaus, which could affect your score negatively. In serious default cases, Yendo could exercise their lien on your vehicle. Always contact Yendo directly if you're having trouble making a payment — most lenders prefer to work with you before escalating.

**Q: Can I apply with an ITIN instead of a Social Security Number?**
> Yendo's current application process requires a US SSN. ITIN applicants should verify current requirements directly at Yendo.com, as their eligibility criteria may evolve.

**Q: How long until my credit score improves?**
> Most users see the first score change within 30–60 days of the card reporting to the bureaus. Meaningful improvement (40–80+ points) typically takes 6–12 months of consistent, on-time payments with low utilization. There's no shortcut — consistency is the only variable.
