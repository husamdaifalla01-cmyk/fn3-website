**Q: I already have a title loan. Can I still get a car-secured credit card?**
A: It depends on whether you have equity in your car. If your car is worth more than the title loan balance (meaning you have positive equity), Yendo may still approve you. However, the existing lien on your title may complicate things — Yendo would need to be in a second lien position or the existing loan would need to be satisfied first. We recommend checking directly with Yendo's eligibility tool to see your situation.

---

**Q: How is a car-secured credit card different from a title loan?**
A: Several key differences: (1) You keep your title — no lien surrender. Yendo places a lien on the title but doesn't hold it. (2) It's revolving credit — you pay it down and reuse it like a regular credit card. (3) APR is much lower (typically 20-35% vs 200-400%+ for title loans). (4) It reports to all 3 credit bureaus, so on-time payments build your credit score. (5) Default protections: credit card default has a longer, more consumer-protective process than title loan repossession.

---

**Q: What's the catch with a car-secured credit card?**
A: The honest answer: Yendo places a lien on your car's title. This means if you default on the credit card (consistently miss payments over months), they can ultimately repossess your vehicle — similar to defaulting on a car loan. Unlike a title loan, this process takes longer and has more consumer protections. If you pay your monthly minimums, nothing ever happens to your car. It's only a risk if you completely stop paying the balance.

---

**Q: Will this hurt my credit score?**
A: The initial eligibility check is a soft inquiry — it won't affect your score. If you proceed with a full application, a hard inquiry may occur. However, once approved, on-time payments will positively impact your credit score over time. Most people with bad credit see meaningful score improvement within 6-12 months of responsible use.

---

**Q: How long until I get my card?**
A: Yendo's current processing times vary. The eligibility check takes 30 seconds. If approved, card delivery is typically 7-10 business days. For exact current timing, check Yendo's website directly — Mintbrooks doesn't have access to their operational details.
