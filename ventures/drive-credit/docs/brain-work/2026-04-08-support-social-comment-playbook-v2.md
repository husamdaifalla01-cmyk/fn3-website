# Mintbrooks — Support: Social Comment Response Playbook v2
**Date:** 2026-04-08 | **Agent:** Head of Support

---

## Purpose
Once TikTok and IG videos are posted, comments will arrive. This playbook prepares Husam (and any future community manager) with pre-written responses.

---

## Comment Categories + Responses

### Category 1: "Is this a scam?"
**Comment type:** "This is too good to be true" / "sounds like a scam"

**Response:**
```
Totally fair skepticism — there are a lot of scams in this space. Yendo is a real Visa credit card (not prepaid, not debit) issued by a licensed lender. They've been operating since 2021, you can find their licensing info on their website. We're an independent comparison site (Mintbrooks) — we don't hold your money or application. Think of us like Bankrate, but specifically for car-secured cards.
```

---

### Category 2: "What's the catch?"
**Comment type:** "What are the fees?" / "what's the catch"

**Response:**
```
Good question — here are the honest downsides:
1. Annual fee (~$75–$100/yr) — higher than basic secured cards
2. APR is higher than prime cards (~24–29%) — typical for secured products
3. Not available in all states — about 35 states right now
4. Your car needs to have no outstanding loan

If those fit your situation, the benefits (no cash deposit, up to $10k limit) can outweigh the cost. We have a full review at mintbrooks.com/finance/yendo-review
```

---

### Category 3: "My car has a loan on it"
**Comment type:** "I still owe on my car" / "I have a car payment"

**Response:**
```
This one only works if your car is fully paid off (no outstanding auto loan) — the lender needs to be the sole lienholder. If you're still paying off your car, a traditional secured card (like Discover Secured or Capital One Secured) is probably your best path. They require a $200–$500 cash deposit but report to all 3 bureaus the same way.
```

---

### Category 4: "What states?"
**Comment type:** "Does this work in [state]?" / "I'm in California"

**Response:**
```
Check the state guide at mintbrooks.com/finance/yendo-states-guide — they're in about 35 states right now. California, Minnesota, and Vermont are unfortunately not included yet. If you're in an excluded state, we have a comparison of alternatives on the same page.
```

---

### Category 5: "I want to apply"
**Comment type:** "How do I apply?" / "Where do I sign up?"

**Response:**
```
Link in bio → mintbrooks.com/finance/qualify — takes about 60 seconds to see if you and your car qualify. It's a soft pull, so it won't affect your credit score just to check. If your car qualifies, you'll be directed to Yendo's application from there.
```

---

### Category 6: Negative/angry
**Comment type:** "Banks are still evil" / "predatory fees"

**Response:**
```
That frustration is valid — the traditional credit system is designed for people who already have credit. Car-secured cards aren't perfect (the fees are real), but they fill a gap that most banks won't touch. If the terms don't work for you, we always recommend checking Discover Secured or the CFPB's credit-building resources as alternatives.
```

---

## Golden Rules
1. Always respond within 24 hours of a comment (algorithm boost from engagement)
2. Never delete skeptical comments — respond and let the thread build credibility
3. Never argue — acknowledge and redirect
4. One link per response max — don't spam
5. Heart every comment that isn't hostile — signals engagement to algorithm
