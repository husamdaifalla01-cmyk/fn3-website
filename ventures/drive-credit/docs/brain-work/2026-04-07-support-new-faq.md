## New FAQ Entry: "How Long Does It Take to Rebuild Credit?"

**Q: How long does it take to rebuild credit?**

Most people with bad credit see meaningful improvement within 6–12 months of consistent
on-time payments on a new positive account. Here is a realistic timeline:

- **Month 1–2:** New account opens, first payment reported. Score may dip slightly (new inquiry), then recover.
- **Month 3–6:** 3–6 on-time payments. Pattern emerging. Most people see 20–40 point improvement from starting score.
- **Month 6–12:** Positive trend clear to lenders. 40–80 point improvement realistic.
- **Month 12–24:** Multiple positive accounts, 12+ months of history. Old negatives aging. 600–650 range achievable from most starting points.

The fastest way to start: open a card that reports to all 3 bureaus monthly and keep utilization below 10%.
If you own a car, a car-secured card requires no cash deposit — you can start today without locking up money.

## New FAQ Entry: "What Happens If I Don't Make Payments on Yendo?"

**Q: What happens if I miss a Yendo payment? Can they take my car?**

This is a common worry and an important distinction: Yendo is NOT a title loan.

With a title loan, your car can be repossessed if you miss a payment. Yendo is a credit card — 
the car's equity is collateral in the sense that it determines your credit limit, but Yendo 
does not hold a lien on your vehicle.

If you miss a Yendo payment:
- You'll incur a late fee
- The missed payment will be reported to the credit bureaus (hurting your score)
- Your account may be charged a penalty APR
- In extreme cases of non-payment, Yendo can close your account and pursue collections

But **they do not repossess your car** for a missed credit card payment. Yendo functions like 
a regular Visa credit card for all billing and collections purposes.

Mintbrooks does not handle Yendo accounts. For payment issues, contact Yendo directly through 
their app or website.

## New FAQ Entry: "What If My State Is Not Available?"

**Q: I'm in [excluded state]. Are there alternatives?**

Yendo is currently available in 37 states + DC. If you're in one of the 14 excluded states 
(AK, HI, IA, LA, ME, MD, MA, MN, MO, NJ, NY, OK, SD, WI), here are the best alternatives:

1. **OpenSky Secured Visa** — available in all 50 states, no credit check, $200 deposit becomes your credit limit. 
2. **Chime Credit Builder** — no deposit, no annual fee, requires direct deposit to a Chime account. Also nationwide.
3. **Self Credit Builder** — combines a credit builder loan with a secured card. Available in most states.

These all report to the 3 major bureaus and will help you build credit while you wait for Yendo's expansion.
