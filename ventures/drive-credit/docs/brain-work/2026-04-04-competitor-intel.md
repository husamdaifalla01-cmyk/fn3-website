## Executive Summary

Mintbrooks operates in the subprime credit card / auto loan affiliate space, a niche dominated by high-authority financial publishers (NerdWallet, Bankrate, LendingTree, Credit Karma) and purpose-built affiliate sites (BadCredit.org, CardRates.com, CompareCards.com). The competitive moat of incumbents is built on domain authority (DR 80+), massive content libraries (thousands of pages), and direct issuer relationships.

**The critical strategic finding:** Yendo, Mintbrooks' primary affiliate offer ($112.50 CPL), just secured an additional $200M from i80 Group (Feb 2026) and is expanding from 36 to 45 states with plans for all 50. This means: (1) more eligible traffic, (2) Yendo is investing heavily in growth and will likely increase marketing spend, creating more consumer awareness, and (3) competitors will start covering Yendo more aggressively as it scales, making "Yendo review" content increasingly competitive.

**The window is now.** Mintbrooks should aggressively capture Yendo-branded search terms and "car equity credit card" long-tail queries before incumbents invest in this sub-niche.

## Key Intelligence Summary

1. **Yendo is scaling fast** — $200M new funding, expanding to 45 states (from 36), plans for all 50. The product is gaining legitimacy. Mintbrooks should ride this wave.

2. **No competitor owns "car equity credit card" as a niche** — NerdWallet/Bankrate cover it as one product among thousands. BadCredit.org doesn't specialize in it. This is Mintbrooks' opportunity to become THE authority.

3. **BadCredit.org (958K pageviews/month) is the template** — they built a massive content moat through credit-score-segmented + product-type-segmented content. Mintbrooks should replicate this playbook within the car-equity sub-niche.

4. **State-specific pages are an uncontested opportunity** — nobody is creating "car equity credit card in Texas" type content. With Yendo's state restrictions being a real consideration for users, this content has genuine search intent.

5. **The $112.50 CPL is exceptionally high** for this niche — most bad credit card affiliate programs pay $20-60. Mintbrooks only needs ~9 leads/month for $1,000 revenue. The math works even at very low traffic if conversion rate is decent.

6. **AI-powered lending is the macro trend** but is not yet a direct threat to affiliate sites — Upstart, Credit Karma, and others use AI for underwriting/matching, not for replacing affiliate content. The affiliate model remains viable for the foreseeable future.
