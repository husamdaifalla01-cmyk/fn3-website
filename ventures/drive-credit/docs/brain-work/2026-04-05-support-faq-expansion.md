## 5 New FAQ Entries (Add to support state)

### Q: Can I get a Yendo card if my car is financed?
**A:** Typically, no. Yendo requires a clear title (no existing lien) so they can place a first-lien position on your vehicle. If you're still making car payments, you generally won't qualify until the loan is paid off.

### Q: What happens to my car if I miss a payment?
**A:** Yendo is a credit card, not a title loan. A single missed payment triggers a late fee and potential interest charges — the same as any credit card. Yendo does not repossess your car for missing one payment. However, sustained non-payment (as with any secured debt) can lead to collection actions over time. The key distinction: this is NOT a title loan with aggressive repossession terms.

### Q: Does Yendo help build credit?
**A:** Yes. Yendo reports to all three major credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) every month. When you pay on time and keep your balance low, it actively improves your credit score. That's the whole point — use it like a debit card (spend $50, pay it off monthly), and your score goes up over time.

### Q: What's the annual fee for Yendo?
**A:** Yendo charges an annual fee. Exact terms change — verify the current fee directly with Yendo during the eligibility check. The $112.50 CPL we earn as affiliates is separate from any fees you'd pay as a cardholder.

### Q: How long does Yendo approval take?
**A:** The eligibility check takes about 30 seconds. If you proceed to a full application, approval typically takes a few business days, and the physical card ships within 7-10 business days after approval.
