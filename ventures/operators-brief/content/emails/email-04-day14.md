**The 3-Number ROI Framework for AI Projects:**

**Number 1: Annual Value Created**

Pick one of these (don't double-count):
- Hours saved × fully-loaded hourly cost of the person doing the task
- Revenue gained from new capacity × gross margin
- Costs eliminated (tools replaced, vendors replaced, headcount not hired)

**Number 2: Total Cost of Implementation**

- One-time build cost (developer time, setup, configuration)
- First-year tool/API costs
- Training and change management time (usually underestimated)

**Number 3: Payback Period**

Number 2 ÷ (Number 1 ÷ 12) = months to payback

If payback is under 3 months, it's a strong project. Under 1 month is exceptional. Over 12 months needs strong strategic justification.

---

**Why most teams get this wrong:**

They calculate the value but forget the true implementation cost. A "free" AI tool that requires 60 hours of developer time at $150/hour isn't free — it's $9,000 before the first dollar of value.

They also underestimate the ongoing cost. Claude API at low volume looks cheap. At production volume with 500 API calls/day, you need to model actual usage.
