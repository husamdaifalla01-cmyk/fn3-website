# Email 02: Day 3

**Subject:** What operators do in their first 30 days with AI
**Preview text:** The pattern I see in operators who move fast vs. operators who stall.

---

Three days in. I want to share something I've noticed from operators who implement quickly vs. operators who read 50 newsletters and implement nothing.

**The operators who move fast do one thing differently:**

They don't try to find "the right AI project." They pick one repeatable task that costs them 3+ hours per week and build one automation. Just one. They don't wait for the perfect use case, the right budget approval, or the right hire.

The operators who stall are waiting for a signal that says "this is the one." That signal doesn't come. You learn by doing one.

---

**Here's a 30-day framework:**

**Week 1:** Audit one department's weekly time. Log every task and the hours spent. Identify the single task that is most repetitive and least creative.

**Week 2:** Find the case study in The Operators Brief that most closely maps to your situation. Use it as your blueprint.

**Week 3:** Build a prototype. Not production. A prototype that one person uses internally. Measure the time savings on 10 examples.

**Week 4:** If the prototype works, productionize. If it doesn't, pick a different task and restart.

That's it. One task. 30 days. Real measurement.

---

**A question worth sitting with:**

What's the single task in your operation that you'd most want to eliminate if you knew it could be done reliably without you?

That's usually where to start.
